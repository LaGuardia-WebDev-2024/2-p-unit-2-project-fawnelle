J-fashion is a variety of styles that stems from Japan, insipired by many different things and is often seen in the streets of Tokyo. In this guide I'll explain a few styles, however there are far too many substyles of styles to go over..and styles in general. So take this guide very generally!

JIRAI KEI ૮꒰◞ ˕ ◟ ྀི꒱ა જ⁀➴

Jirai Kei is one of the most popular styles seen in Japan, lots of brands have been creating clothing for it as of recent. The word Jirai directly translates to "landmine", the style intended to be a mockery of emotional girls, and turned it into an aesthetic. The style consists of mainly pink, white and black; skirts, dresses, blouse, knee socks and platforms. It's often paired with frilly accesories and Pien makeup; a style of makeup that accentiuates the eyes.

GYARU {ギャル}☆⌒(ゝ。∂)

Gyaru is another style that's gained attention recently, known for its big personality, large hair, nails and overall statement pieces. There's lots of substyles of Gyaru such as ;Kogal, Himegal, Rokku Gyaru, Yamamba, and more! A big part of the style is the mindset as well, with a carefree and rebellious spirit.

DECORA KEI {デコラ}

Decora Kei is a style consisting very colorful clothing and the aspect of overaccesorizing, using hairclips, jewerly and even stickers! The word "Decora" comes from the english word Decorate, accurate to what the style is. I've spotted a few students at Lag who wear this style really well!!

VKEI {ヴィジュアル系 }

Visual Kei is an artistic movement among Japanese music artists, as well as an umbrella term used for the style that is worn by both the bands and fans. This style is strongly music based, similar to how some styles in the west align with music genre.

LOLITA {ロリータ・ファッション}

Lolita Fashion, while often confused with the book... (please dont do that!!!!!!) is a style originated in Japan inspired by Victorian and the Roccoco era of clothing. Commonly seen is frills and bows, no limit of color palette but often seen worn with pastels. Lolita's refer to their outfits as coord, with a blouse, skirt/dress, headwear, socks, shoes, wrist cuffs and accesories that usually match.
